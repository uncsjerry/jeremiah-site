## PART 3: Johari Window Teachback

### Question 6A: Johari Window — What It Is & Why Forum Uses It (2 minutes)

**Key message:** The Johari Window explains why Forum works — the entire process is designed to expand the Open Area through feedback, vulnerability, and exploration.

**Concepts to cover:**
- Created by Joseph Luft and Harrington Ingham, 1955
- 2x2 grid, four quadrants:
  - **Open Area** — known to you and others (public self)
  - **Blind Spot** — others see it, you can't (patterns, strengths you take for granted)
  - **Hidden Area** — you know it, others don't (fears, insecurities, private truths — where vulnerability lives)
  - **Unknown** — neither you nor others are aware (untapped potential, unconscious patterns)
- **Why Forum uses it:** Forum is designed to expand the Open Area
  - Presentations + feedback → Blind Spot shrinks
  - Sharing what you carry alone → Hidden Area shrinks
  - Group safety + deep exploration → Unknown begins to reveal itself
- The goal isn't perfection — it's expansion. More open window = more authentic leadership, relationships, and life.

**Consider using a visual:** Draw or show the 2x2 grid. The application says visuals are encouraged.

**What evaluators are scoring:**
- Model depth and versatility — do you really understand it?
- Clear, concise, complete explanation
- Connection with the audience — are you teaching, not reciting?

**Timing:** 2 minutes — introduce the model and its origin (15 sec), walk through all four quadrants (45 sec), connect to Forum specifically (45 sec), land the "why it matters" (15 sec).

That capability was in the Unknown until you put yourself in a situation that demanded it.

**Closing thought:** Every Forum meeting is an act of expanding the Open Area. That expansion is what makes Forum transformative.

**What evaluators are scoring:**

- All four quadrants illustrated

- Authenticity

**Timing:** 3 minutes — set the scene of where you started (30 sec), Hidden Area story (45 sec), Blind Spot story with the feedback moment (45 sec), Unknown story with Forum for Business (30 sec), tie it all together (30 sec).

## PART 4: Forum Scenarios — Response Framework

Part 4 gives you three scenarios in real time with 3 minutes to think and 1 take to record. You can't script these — but you can prepare how you think.

### Your Response Framework
1. **Pause and name what you see** — "What I'm noticing in this scenario is..."
2. **Identify the Forum principle at play** — confidentiality, psychological safety, no advice-giving, gestalt, etc.
3. **Describe what you would do** — specific facilitator action
4. **Explain why** — connect it back to the group's growth and safety

### Key Phrases You Use Naturally
- "What are we solving for?"
- "I'm noticing that..."
- "Let's pause and check in with the group."
- "The goal here isn't to fix — it's to understand."
- "What would serve the group right now?"
- "How does this connect to what you shared earlier?"

### Principles to Demonstrate
- **Forum ≠ therapy, coaching, or a board meeting** — show you know the distinction
- **Facilitator vs. Moderator vs. Coach** — you are the facilitator; show you know the boundaries
- **Authenticity over perfection** — evaluators say there's no single "right" answer
- **Psychological safety first** — every response should center group safety
- **Confidentiality is sacred** — if the scenario involves sensitive information, name that

### What Evaluators Are Scoring
- Relevance to Forum and the issues at hand
- Understanding of the CFF role (facilitator, not moderator or coach)
- Authenticity — does the response give insight into your style and skillset?
- Clarity — does the audience understand your response and thought process?
